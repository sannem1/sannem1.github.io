What I do now

I am currently a third-year computer science student at the University of Kansas. I'm a member of the SELF Program, a competitive engineering leadership program that focuses on building leadership and management skills. I hold the position of President for the Outreach Committee, and I am in charge of reaching out to schools all over Kansas and advertizing the program. Additionally, I'm part of the Catalyst Program, which provides a platform for students to develop their ventures with industry insights. My current venture is an LLC aimed at helping students gain experience through contract work and internships by connecting them with companies. I am also the President of Women In Computing at KU, a club that offers community and professional development opportunities to women in tech. Recently, I spearheaded a new program designed to teach women essential skills that aren't covered in-depth in coursework but are crucial for success in the tech industry.
